## What to build

Make a *touching* pencil win over direct touches in the Apple shell's input
routing (PRD rules 1–2), so a resting palm can neither block, cut, nor
pollute a pencil stroke. Works on every pencil-capable iPad — no hover
hardware involved.

- **Pencil begin is always admitted.** Down direct touches (palm, resting
  fingers) no longer block a pencil begin. An in-flight direct-finger stroke
  resolves through the same semantics as the Gesture Signal: a dragged
  stroke commits what it drew (one history entry); a stroke still holding
  only its Deferred Begin is discarded (no pixels, no history entry).
- **An active pencil stroke ignores direct touches entirely.** Any number of
  direct touches may begin, move, and end during a pencil stroke without
  drawing, without firing the Gesture Signal, and without ending the stroke
  — extending 245's lone-resting-finger tolerance to the full palm. Only
  the Originating Touch (the pencil) drives the stroke.
- **Viewport gestures are suppressed during a pencil stroke.** The
  pinch-zoom and two-finger-pan recognizers must not begin while a pencil
  stroke is active; they resume once the pencil lifts.
- The episode rule ("no stroke until every touch lifts") and all
  finger-only behavior are unchanged.
- Register the extended pencil-priority semantics in the domain vocabulary
  (extends the Gesture Signal / Originating Touch entries), as 245 did.

The routing decisions stay in the platform-neutral routing seam (pure,
UIKit-free, generic over touch identity); the view layer only feeds events,
executes returned commands, and gates the recognizers.

## Acceptance criteria

- With one or two direct touches already down, a pencil begin starts a
  stroke normally at the pencil's location.
- A dragged finger stroke in flight when the pencil begins commits exactly
  what it drew before the pencil landed, as one history entry; the pencil
  stroke then proceeds independently.
- A finger stroke still holding only its Deferred Begin when the pencil
  begins leaves canvas pixels and history untouched (no stray dot).
- During an active pencil stroke, direct touches — one or several — never
  paint, never end the stroke, and never fire the Gesture Signal; the
  stroke's samples contain only the pencil's coordinates.
- During an active pencil stroke, pinch-zoom and two-finger pan do not
  operate; after the pencil lifts, both gestures work again.
- After a pencil stroke ends with direct touches still down, no new stroke
  begins until every touch lifts (episode rule preserved).
- Finger-only interaction (Deferred Begin, Gesture Signal, tap-to-dot,
  episode rule) behaves exactly as before; existing routing tests stay
  green.
- New behavior is pinned by Swift Testing tests at the routing seam via the
  existing routed-editor harness, asserting pixels + history outcomes (no
  event-forwarding call-sequence assertions).
- The domain vocabulary document reflects the pencil-priority rules.
- macOS target builds clean; macOS input paths untouched.
