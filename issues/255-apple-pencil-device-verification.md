## What to build

A manual on-device verification pass for the pencil work (issues 252–254).
The simulator cannot simulate pencil hover, real palm contact geometry, or
gesture-recognizer interplay — this is the human-in-the-loop closure the
PRD's testing decisions call for. It also folds in the pinch/pan interplay
pass that issue 245 left outstanding.

Run on a hover-capable iPad (Pro M2+ with Pencil 2 / Pencil Pro); if a
non-hover pencil iPad is available, run the contact-priority subset there
too.

### Checklist

Palm rejection (contact):

- [ ] Rest a palm, then draw with the pencil — the stroke starts and
      follows the pencil only.
- [ ] Draw a long pencil line while the palm rests/shifts (one and multiple
      contact points) — the line never cuts off, no stray marks, one undo
      entry per stroke.
- [ ] During a pencil stroke, deliberate two-finger pinch/pan does nothing;
      after the pencil lifts, pinch-zoom and two-finger pan work again.
- [ ] After a pencil stroke with the palm still down, lift everything —
      the next single finger draws normally.
- [ ] Finger-only drawing, tap-to-dot, pinch-zoom, and two-finger pan all
      behave as before (245 regression check, including the pinch/pan
      interplay pass 245 deferred).

Hover:

- [ ] Hovering shows the target-cell highlight, tracking the pencil live;
      it disappears on touch-down, on leaving hover range, and off-canvas.
- [ ] The highlight stays glued to the correct cell across zoom and pan.
- [ ] While hovering, a landing finger or palm draws nothing; two fingers
      still pinch/pan; pencil touch-down draws immediately.
- [ ] Hover feels responsive at 120Hz displays (no visible lag or hitching).
